Within this ecosystem, my work is configured as a **Critical Speculation Space**.

## üß¨ Research Axes

My contribution to SK-Crossroads is organized into five vectors, bridging hard engineering with philosophy of technology and creative experimentation:

### 1. Counterfactual Worlds & AI Hermeneutics
Investigating how to expand the conception of reading and signification (hermeneutics) in AI models, moving beyond statistical token prediction.

* **The Experiment:** Blending Causal Models (Causal Inference) with Transformer architectures.
* **The Goal:** To increase creativity and Explainability, grounded in diverse analogies of human understanding.
* **The Question:** How can we use AI to reason about *"What if?"* and generate alternative realities that enrich our view of the possible?

### 2. New Chronotopes for Meaning Making
Inspired by Mikhail Bakhtin, we investigate how AI perceives and constructs time and space.

* **The Critique:** Current AI operates in linear, compressed time, often ignoring the cyclical nature of non-Western cultures.
* **The Goal:** To create **"Algorhythms"** ‚Äî technologies anchored in alternative cultural matrices, where time is relational and space is one of care and reciprocity.
* **The Practice:** Cultural Analytics to map and correct temporal biases in large datasets.

### 3. Socio-Cognitive Agents (Beyond Behaviorism)
Questioning the hegemony of Behaviorism (stimulus-response) implicit in most Reinforcement Learning (RL) systems.

* **The Action:** Building Agent Models based on Socio-Cognitive theories (Vygotsky).
* **The Shift:** Moving from training focused solely on individual reward maximization to agents that learn through social interaction, mediation, and Zones of Proximal Development (ZPD).
* **The Stack:** Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (MARL), Social Simulation Environments.

### 4. Technological Mediation & Ethical AI Design
Centering Indigenous Protocols and Artificial Intelligence to amplify historically silenced voices and perspectives.

* **The Context:** Challenging hegemonic narratives by prioritizing Sovereignty and Self-Determination over data (UNDRIP).
* **The Ethics:** Shifting from extractive logic to Reciprocity and Care, viewing technologies as relations that demand mutual responsibility.
* **The Vision:** Digital Decolonization and Epistemic Diversity‚Äîmoving from standard Algorithms to *Algorhythms*, recognizing a pluriverse of valid knowledge systems.

### 5. Anthropophagic AI & Post-Sampling Aesthetics
A laboratory for creative experimentation inspired by the Brazilian Modernist concept of *Antropofagia* (Oswald de Andrade) and contemporary remix culture.

* **The Concept:** "Devouring" global, hegemonic AI models to digest them and produce something uniquely local, critical, and sovereign.
* **The Practice:** Exploring Post-Sampling techniques‚Äîtreating generative AI outputs not as final products, but as raw material for sampling, glitching, and re-signifying.
* **The Goal:** To reclaim agency over the "black box" through artistic misuse and poetic engineering.
